What we're watching, and why

This child had necrotising enterocolitis as a newborn and, at three months, surgery that removed a large part of the bowel (short bowel syndrome), followed by treated neonatal sepsis. Since toddlerhood there has been recurring joint pain in the legs, knees and feet, plus ongoing trouble with stool consistency and control.

The pattern noticed

Joint-pain days seem to line up with harder tummy days, and both appear to worsen after high-sugar days. Acknowledging the pain (warmth, comfort) seems to ease it.

🧪
The hypothesis to test: that an imbalanced gut microbiome after surgery may drive low-grade inflammation showing up both as gut symptoms and joint pain. A reasonable idea to explore with doctors — not a settled fact.
⚠️
Blood in stool, a hot/swollen joint, fever with joint pain, weight loss, or pain that won't settle → contact a doctor. Not "track and wait" signs.
